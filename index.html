Why now

The sci-fi part finally shipped.

For years a real Jarvis was impossible. In the last two it stopped being a question of "if" and became a question of "how well." Four breakthroughs converged — and Jarvis is built on all of them.

Sub-second voice

On-device speech models (Whisper, Parakeet) now turn your words to text and back in a heartbeat — fast enough to feel like a conversation, not a command.

Local-first brains

Capable models run on a laptop or a Mac mini. The reasoning that used to need a data center can now live entirely on your machine.

One open tool standard

MCP became the way assistants plug into apps and devices. Smart tool selection means Jarvis can hold hundreds of tools without slowing down.

Memory that sticks

Persistent, personal context is now a first-class building block — so an assistant can actually know your world instead of resetting every chat.

Connect everything

One assistant, wired into your world.

Jarvis acts through an open tool layer (MCP) with scoped, revocable permissions — so it reaches your apps, your devices, and your home. [NEED FROM CLIENT: confirm the real, launch-ready integration list]

Questions

The things people ask first.

Is it actually private?
Yes — that's the point. Jarvis can run entirely on your own machine, so your voice, files, and messages never have to leave it. When you do use a cloud model for a harder request, secrets like passwords and tokens are redacted first. Your assistant, your data, your rules.
How fast does it respond?
Fast enough to feel like talking to a person — well under a second from your voice to its reply on a typical setup. The speech and reasoning run locally, so there's no round-trip lag to a far-off server. [target — final latency confirmed at launch]
What can it connect to?
Your calendar, email, messages, notes, files, music, smart home, browser, and your desktop apps — through an open tool layer (MCP) with scoped, revocable permissions. If a tool speaks MCP or has an API, Jarvis can likely use it. [NEED FROM CLIENT: confirm launch integrations]
Do I need to be technical?
No. The goal is "say the word and it works." Setup walks you through connecting your apps and choosing what Jarvis is allowed to do. Power users can go deep with custom tools and local models; everyone else just talks to it.
What does it run on?
A modern laptop or desktop is enough to run Jarvis locally, with more horsepower unlocking bigger on-device models. You can also point it at a cloud model when you want maximum capability. [NEED FROM CLIENT: confirm supported platforms — Mac / Windows / Linux]

No. Jarvis is inspired by the idea everyone fell in love with on screen — an assistant that talks, acts, and has your back — but it's an independent product, not affiliated with or endorsed by Marvel or Disney. [NEED FROM CLIENT: confirm final product name & legal review]
